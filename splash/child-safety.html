Child Safety Standards

Last updated: April 10, 2026

Feels is committed to protecting children from sexual abuse, exploitation, and harm. This page outlines our standards, prevention practices, and reporting procedures in compliance with global child safety laws and Google Play's Child Safety Standards Policy.

Feels is strictly an 18+ adults-only app. Minors are prohibited from creating accounts or using any part of the service. Anyone discovered to be under 18 will have their account immediately and permanently terminated.

1. Our Commitment

Feels has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse material (CSAM) and any form of child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). We:

  • Prohibit users under 18 from creating accounts or accessing any feature.
  • Detect, prevent, and remove CSAM from our platform.
  • Report all suspected CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and applicable regional authorities.
  • Cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations.
  • Permanently ban any account associated with CSAM, child grooming, or attempts to access minors.

2. Age Verification & Account Creation

Feels requires every user to:

  • Confirm they are at least 18 years old at sign-up.
  • Provide a date of birth that is verified against our 18+ requirement.
  • Agree to our Terms of Service, which prohibit underage use.
  • Submit photos for verification — accounts with photos depicting minors are immediately rejected.

If we have reason to believe a user is under 18, we will lock the account, remove their content, and permanently ban the user.

3. Content Moderation & Detection

Feels uses a combination of automated and human moderation to detect harmful content:

  • Photo screening: All profile photos are scanned for CSAM signatures using industry-standard hash-matching tools.
  • Profile review: Profiles flagged by users or our systems are reviewed by trained moderators.
  • Message monitoring: Reports of inappropriate messages trigger human review. End-to-end encryption protects user privacy, but reported messages can be reviewed via our reporting flow.
  • Keyword filtering: Profiles and messages containing language associated with grooming or child exploitation are flagged automatically.

4. User Reporting

Feels provides in-app tools for users to report safety concerns, including suspected child exploitation, grooming, or CSAM. From any profile or conversation, users can:

  • Tap the menu icon and select Report.
  • Choose a reason (including "Underage", "Inappropriate photos", "Harassment", "Spam/Fake", or "Other").
  • Provide additional context.
  • Block and unmatch instantly.

Reports of suspected child safety violations are escalated to our trust and safety team within 24 hours and to law enforcement when warranted.

5. Reporting CSAM

If you encounter content on Feels that you believe constitutes child sexual abuse material or child exploitation, please report it immediately:

In-app

Tap the report button on the user's profile or conversation, select an appropriate reason, and submit. Reports are reviewed within 24 hours.

By email

Email safety@feelsfun.app with as much detail as possible. Reports to this address are monitored daily by our safety team.

To authorities

You can also report directly to:

  • NCMEC CyberTipline: cybertipline.org or 1-800-843-5678
  • Internet Watch Foundation (UK/EU): iwf.org.uk
  • Your local law enforcement

6. Compliance & Reporting to Authorities

Feels complies with all applicable child safety laws, including:

  • U.S. federal law requiring electronic service providers to report apparent CSAM to NCMEC.
  • EU regulations on illegal content moderation and reporting.
  • Other regional and national laws governing online child safety.

We maintain a policy of immediate reporting of all suspected CSAM to NCMEC and the relevant national authorities. We preserve evidence in accordance with legal requirements and cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations.

7. Designated Point of Contact

Our designated point of contact for child safety matters and CSAM/CSAE compliance is:

Email: safety@feelsfun.app

Response time: Within 24 hours for child safety matters.

This contact is staffed by a member of our team trained to discuss CSAM prevention practices, content moderation policies, and compliance with reporting obligations.

8. Account Consequences

Any account found to violate our child safety standards will be:

  • Immediately and permanently banned.
  • Reported to NCMEC and relevant law enforcement.
  • Have all associated content removed and preserved as evidence per legal requirements.
  • Blocked from creating new accounts on Feels.

9. Updates to This Policy

We review and update this policy regularly as our practices evolve and as new laws and standards emerge. Material changes will be communicated through our app and on this page.

10. Questions

For questions about this policy or our child safety practices, contact safety@feelsfun.app.
